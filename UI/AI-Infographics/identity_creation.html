The Access Model: A Two-Tiered System

Businesses can access these repositories through two distinct pathways, each with different levels of complexity and investment.

Path 1: Direct Integration

Become a Licensed Agency (AUA/KUA)

High cost, high friction. Requires significant investment in tech, compliance, and security audits. Best for large, regulated entities.

OR

Path 2: Third-Party APIs

Use a Verification Provider

Low cost, low friction. Fast to market by leveraging a provider's existing license and infrastructure. Ideal for most businesses.

Comparing Verification Friction

The method of verification varies significantly across documents, impacting speed and ease of integration. API-based methods offer real-time, low-friction validation, while manual or portal-based checks are more complex and slower for businesses to scale.

The Legal Framework

Identity validation is governed by a robust legal framework. The Aadhaar Act provides the foundation, while the DPDP Act introduces strict data privacy rules. Crucially, Supreme Court rulings have defined the boundaries for commercial use, making Aadhaar voluntary for private services.

  • Aadhaar Act, 2016: Provides legal backing for the UID project.
  • DPDP Act, 2023: Mandates user consent and data security.
  • Supreme Court Rulings: Limit mandatory Aadhaar use in the private sector.
  • KYC/AML Regulations: Define rules for financial institutions.

A Critical Distinction: Identity vs. Citizenship

A landmark Bombay High Court ruling clarified that possessing documents like Aadhaar, PAN, or Voter ID is proof of identity for availing services, but not definitive proof of Indian citizenship.
